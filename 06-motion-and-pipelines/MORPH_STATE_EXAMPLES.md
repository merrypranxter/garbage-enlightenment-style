# MORPH STATE EXAMPLES

This file gives example state chains for metamorphosis in Garbage Enlightenment.

These are not rigid storyboards.
They are examples of the kind of symbolic substitutions and transition logic the style likes.

## Core principle

A good morph chain should:
- preserve contour continuity where possible
- reassign meaning quickly

- let intermediate states matter
- keep some family resemblance between states
- feel symbolically alive, not random

## EXAMPLE CHAIN 1 — EYE TO MOUTH TO CREATURE

### States
1. floating eye
2. eye stretches horizontally
3. iris becomes mouth cavity
4. lashes become teeth or lip edges
5. black cavity expands
6. tiny limbs emerge
7. mouth-creature appears
8. creature collapses back into eye shape

### Why it works
- strong body glyph continuity
- fast semantic reassignment
- easy loop logic
- very LSD-logic adjacent without needing realism

## EXAMPLE CHAIN 2 — HAND TO PORTAL TO FACE

### States
1. simple palm glyph
2. palm lines become contour rings
3. palm center opens into dark circular cavity
4. cavity becomes portal
5. fingers curl into facial contour
6. eye appears in palm-center/portal space
7. hand-face hybrid resolves
8. face dissolves back into ringed hand symbol

### Why it works
- mock esoterica + body glyph fusion
- strong central icon staging
- fake revelation tone

## INTERMEDIATE STATE RULES

Important:
The most stylistically alive moment may be:
- the almost-eye
- the half-mouth
- the contour that could still be either face or field
- the cute thing just starting to go wrong
- the creature not fully committed yet

Do not design only the endpoints.

Design the semantically unstable middle.

## LSD-LOGIC NOTE

These morph chains resemble **cartoonized LSD-hallucination logic** because they allow:
- one shape to imply several meanings
- fast significance reassignment
- visible over-meaning
- recursive identity pressure
- form-to-symbol-to-being transitions

Again, the point is not realism.
The point is translating that unstable logic into rude symbolic cartoon motion.

## FINAL RULE

A good morph chain should feel like:
**the image is changing its mind about what it is, but each new decision still grows out of the
last one**

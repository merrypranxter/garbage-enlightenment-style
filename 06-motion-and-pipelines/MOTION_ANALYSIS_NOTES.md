# MOTION ANALYSIS NOTES

This file collects practical notes for analyzing motion references in Garbage Enlightenment.

This matters because movement is one of the style’s deepest identity layers.

## Core principle

When analyzing motion in this style, do not only ask:
“What moved?”

Ask:
- what changed meaning?
- what became what?
- what contour remained?
- what intermediate states mattered?
- what symbolic job got reassigned?

## PRIMARY ANALYSIS QUESTIONS

### 1. What is the base form?
Identify the initial legible unit:
- eye
- blob
- face
- heart

- field
- hand
- creature

### 2. What is the target form?
What does it become, even temporarily?

### 3. What contour survives?
This tells you the morph logic.

### 4. What semantic role changes?
Examples:
- eye → portal
- rainbow → mouth
- blob → creature
- hand → ritual sign

### 5. Which intermediate states matter?
Often these are the most important part.

## THINGS TO TRACK IN FAST MORPHS

- feature migration
- shape stretching

- contour band reinterpretation
- body-glyph reveal timing
- repeated symbolic beats
- reabsorption timing
- alternate readings

## USEFUL MOTION CATEGORIES

### Morph-driven
One form continuously becomes another.

### Reveal-driven
A hidden figure or glyph becomes legible.

### Wobble-driven
A form stays mostly itself but its instability becomes expressive.

### Swarm-driven
Many small units pulse, split, or vary around a shared logic.

### Figure-field driven
A figure emerges from or collapses into abstraction.

## WHAT MAKES A MOTION REF HIGH VALUE

A motion ref is especially useful when it shows:
- strong contour inheritance
- rapid symbolic reassignment
- meaningful semi-states
- body-pattern crossover
- cute-to-gross shift
- cheap dream cadence
- slight recurrence with difference

## WEAK MOTION REFS

Weak refs are mostly:
- simple translation
- normal walk cycle
- boring idle bob
- camera movement without symbolic change
- abstract motion with no emergence pressure

These may still be useful for timing texture, but not for core identity.

## LSD-LOGIC NOTE

Motion refs are especially on-brand when they show **cartoonized LSD-hallucination structure**
such as:

- one shape reading as several things in quick succession
- significance spikes during the morph
- a symbol that becomes too meaningful too fast
- unstable figure-ground identity
- emotional charge carried by transitional states

Again, the value is structural, not documentary.

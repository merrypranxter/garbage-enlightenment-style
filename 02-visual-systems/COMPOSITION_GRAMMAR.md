# COMPOSITION GRAMMAR
Garbage Enlightenment compositions are rarely built like conventional polished illustration.
They are usually driven by:
- icon staging
- symbolic emphasis
- awkward balance
- field pressure
- decorative intrusion
- mutational possibility
- figure–pattern leakage
The composition should support the feeling that the image is:
- unstable
- charged
- readable
- a little rude
- able to morph into another state
## Core principle
Compose for:
- symbolic force
- strange readability
- mutational tension
- flat graphic impact
Not for:
- classical realism
- elegant depth illusion
- perfect perspective
- cinematic polish
- tasteful design restraint
## Main composition modes

--## 1. CENTRAL ICON MODE
A single dominant form occupies the frame.
Examples:
- eye symbol
- hand glyph
- face-creature
- pyramid icon
- head mutation
- portal ring image
### Why it works
This mode helps:
- symbolic readability
- poster strength
- occult parody intensity
- easy mutation tracking in animation
### Good support elements
- halos
- stars
- contour fields
- smaller repeated glyphs
- simple decorative framing
--## 2. FLOATING GLYPH FIELD
The frame contains several detached symbolic units.
Examples:
- eyes
- mouths
- stars
- teeth
- little creatures
- hands
- skulls
- cute symbols

- fake ritual icons
### Good effect
Feels like:
- a sticker sheet from a broken revelation
- dream catalog logic
- body-symbol weather
- animated substitution space
This mode is excellent for playful density.
--## 3. CREATURE TABLEAU
One or more freak beings occupy a simple scene-space.
But “scene” here can be minimal.
The environment often exists only enough to:
- hold the creature
- frame the symbol
- support emotional tone
This mode works best when the creature remains:
- iconically clear
- slightly awkward
- transformation-ready
--## 4. PATTERN-DOMINANT FIELD
The abstract engine takes over the frame.
This can include:
- contour bands
- plasma fields
- thermal melts
- hallucination topographies
- liquid signal maps
### Important rule
Even here, the composition should often suggest:
- latent body

- hidden face
- symbolic emergence
- creature potential
Do not let abstract space become generic wallpaper.
--## 5. FIGURE–PATTERN HYBRID
One of the strongest modes in the repo.
The figure and the field are not fully separate.
Examples:
- head emerging from contour bands
- eye inside rainbow topology
- creature dissolving into plasma
- body parts embedded in decorative psychedelic field
- cute motifs trapped in abstract signal space
This is often the most “true” mode of the style.
## Spatial logic
This style does not require realistic depth.
It often benefits from:
- shallow stage space
- floating forms
- flat color grounds
- symbolic rather than environmental depth
- aura-space instead of perspective-space
Depth can exist, but it should not become the main event.
## Balance logic
Good composition often uses:
- intentional awkwardness
- asymmetrical pressure
- clusters offset by emptiness
- unstable visual weight
- icon + orbiting detail
- decorative interruptions

- repeating mini-forms
The composition should often feel:
- held together
but also
- ready to slip
## Empty space
Empty space can be very useful.
It helps:
- isolate glyphs
- intensify icon-reading
- make floating elements stranger
- support sticker / emblem logic
- keep the image from getting too muddy
## Density
Dense compositions are also valid, especially when they feel like:
- swarm logic
- symbolic overload
- repeated motif weather
- hallucination clutter
- decorative body chaos
The key is that density should still contain motif intelligence.
## Framing devices
Common useful framing elements:
- aura rings
- halos
- contour fields
- repeated stars
- rainbow arcs
- border-like motif clusters
- portal shapes
- repeated body glyph units
These help tie together:
- central icon mode
- mock esoterica
- sweet corruption

- abstract fields
## Animation-aware composition
Because this style often moves through morphing, composition should allow:
- shape inheritance
- readable transition paths
- local transformation zones
- emergence from background
- substitution within the same silhouette field
Good compositional question:
**Can this image become another image without fully re-staging itself?**
If yes, good.
## LSD-hallucination relation
Some compositions in this style resemble **cartoonized LSD-hallucination structure** in the
sense that:
- one shape suggests several meanings at once
- the frame feels full of over-significance
- forms seem ready to reveal hidden forms
- pattern and figure trade places
- the whole image behaves like a symbolic pressure field
Again, the goal is not realism.
The goal is to capture that unstable logic through rude cartoon means.
## Drift warnings
### Too elegant
Bad.
### Too centered and dead
Bad unless central icon tension is strong.
### Too crowded with meaningless junk
Bad.
### Too realistic in environment-building
Bad.
### Too empty with no symbolic charge

Bad.
## Final rule
A good Garbage Enlightenment composition should feel like:
**a flat symbolic stage where creatures, body glyphs, cute motifs, and fake-cosmic signs are all
trying to become each other without losing legibility**

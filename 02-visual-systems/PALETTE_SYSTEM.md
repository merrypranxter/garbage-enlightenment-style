# PALETTE SYSTEM
Garbage Enlightenment has a strong color identity, but that identity should not be oversimplified
into “just use bright rainbow colors.”
The palette system is specific:
- synthetic
- acid
- cartoon-flat
- chemically fake
- rude
- emotionally unstable
- sometimes sweet
- sometimes gross
- sometimes ominous
- sometimes hallucinatory

## Core principle
The base palette should come from the style’s own native ecosystem.
Do not force unrelated palette preferences into the foundation layer.
Optional remix palettes can be added later.
## Main palette behaviors
The style often uses:
- flat or semi-flat color blocks
- high symbolic contrast
- synthetic hue clashes
- candy-to-toxic transitions
- black outline anchoring
- occasional deadened support tones
- sudden, rude pops of hot color
- fields that feel chemically lit rather than naturally lit
## Core native color families
--## 1. ACID BRIGHTS
These are some of the style’s strongest “charge” colors.
### Includes
- acid yellow
- electric cyan
- hot pink
- synthetic red
- hard chartreuse
- toy blue
- toxic green
- bright orange accents
### Best use
- central symbols
- contour-band fields
- eyes, mouths, and body glyph accents
- mock-esoteric icon emphasis
- emotional spike points

--## 2. FLESH / BODY ADJACENT TONES
These keep the style from becoming generic rainbow wallpaper.
### Includes
- weird peach
- pink-beige flesh
- bruise-ish mauves
- lip reds
- tongue pinks
- gum-like reds
- yellowed bone-ish neutrals
### Best use
- mouths
- skin-like blobs
- soft bodies
- mutant creatures
- skulls
- bodily contamination inside otherwise bright compositions
These are crucial because they tie color back to the body.
--## 3. CHEAP SWEETNESS COLORS
These support Sweet Corruption mode.
### Includes
- candy pink
- bubblegum tones
- sweet sky blue
- sticker lavender
- toy teal
- soft rainbow accents
- decorative heart/star colors
### Important note
These should not become too pristine or too polished.
They should still feel slightly cheap, loud, weird, or contaminated.

--## 4. TOXIC / GROSS ACCENTS
These help prevent the style from becoming too pretty.
### Includes
- sickly green
- bile yellow
- moldy teal
- bruise-purple accents
- dirty orange
- harsh red-black interaction
- weird shadow greens
These do not have to dominate.
Even small use can keep the style honest.
--## 5. DEADENED SUPPORT TONES
Not every part of the image should scream.
Useful support tones:
- dirty cream
- dull gray
- flat black
- muted tan
- off-white
- weak blue-gray
- dry bone neutral
- muddy mauve
These help:
- isolate bright forms
- keep contrast readable
- avoid full-spectrum mush
- give symbols room to breathe
## Color relationships
The style often likes:
- hot vs sick

- sweet vs toxic
- flesh vs neon
- black outline vs loud fill
- rainbow field vs one deadened support area
- cute decorative color next to a bodily cavity
This is not refined harmony.
It is strategically rude harmony.
## Palette modes
--## 1. BODY-SYMBOL MODE
Prioritize:
- flesh tones
- mouth reds
- eye whites or pale fills
- black cavities
- hot accent colors
- minor sweet support notes
This mode is strong for:
- faces
- creature portraits
- floating body glyphs
--## 2. MOCK-ESOTERIC MODE
Prioritize:
- black
- acid yellow
- hot pink
- synthetic blue
- toxic green
- symbolic red
- aura-ring contrasts
This mode is good for:
- eye pyramids
- hand glyphs

- portal iconography
- fake revelation graphics
--## 3. SWEET CORRUPTION MODE
Prioritize:
- candy pink
- sweet sky blue
- rainbow accents
- stars/hearts colors
- toy-ish saturated hues
- soft but synthetic decorative notes
Then contaminate with:
- body reds
- gross greens
- black outlines
- asymmetrical ugly color interruptions
--## 4. ABSTRACT HALLUCINATION MODE
Prioritize:
- multi-band contour colors
- thermal transitions
- oil-slick ranges
- false topographic color zoning
- rainbow logic with pressure
But avoid:
- generic smooth rainbow mush
- default psychedelic wallpaper logic
## LSD-hallucination relation
This style can resemble **cartoonized LSD-hallucination color logic** in the sense that:
- colors may feel hyper-significant
- boundaries between zones become emotionally charged
- hue shifts may suggest identity shifts
- rainbow behavior may act like symbolic pressure rather than decoration
- synthetic contrast can feel revelatory for no sensible reason

Again, the repo is not trying to imitate literal perception faithfully.
It is translating that kind of exaggerated significance into rude cartoon color structure.
## What to avoid
### Too tasteful
- muted design palettes
- elegant contemporary restraint
- overly cohesive interior-design color logic
### Too pure rainbow
- undifferentiated spectral slop
- every color at once with no symbolic hierarchy
### Too pastel-cute
- nursery softness
- harmless kawaii candy polish
- color with no weirdness or body tension
### Too grim
- monochrome darkness
- desaturated horror palettes
- gloom without chemical charge
## Prompt guidance
Useful palette phrases:
- synthetic acid cartoon palette
- rude candy-toxic contrast
- flesh tones colliding with neon bands
- cheap psychedelic color zoning
- toy-sweet hues contaminated by body colors
- black-outlined chemical brights
- hallucinatory contour-band palette
- sweet-and-sick cartoon chroma
## Final rule
Color in Garbage Enlightenment should feel like:
**it has been chosen by a mutant sticker god with a bodily sense of humor and a mild cosmic
drug problem**

# LINEWORK AND SIMPLICITY
Garbage Enlightenment depends heavily on graphic clarity.
Its weirdness becomes stronger, not weaker, when the drawing language stays relatively simple.
This style does not need virtuoso rendering.
It needs rude intelligence.
## Core principle
The line should:
- contain
- clarify
- symbolize
- cheapen
- unify
- slightly disrespect the image
The line should not:
- over-polish
- over-describe
- become precious
- become ornamental for its own sake
## Main line qualities
### Common traits
- black or very dark outline
- thin-to-medium weight
- direct shape containment
- simple edge logic
- occasional wobble or hand-done roughness
- cartoon confidence without over-refinement
### Emotional effect
Good linework can make the image feel:
- low-fi
- bodily
- toy-like
- iconic
- funny
- slightly cheap

- mutation-ready
## What line is doing in this style
Line is not just decorative.
It often performs several jobs at once:
- separates symbolic units
- keeps mutation legible
- supports sticker/emblem readability
- strengthens flatness
- makes weird anatomy easier to accept
- protects the image from drifting into painterly slop
- helps the composition survive color chaos
## Simplicity as force multiplier
The style often gets stronger when:
- forms are simplified
- anatomy is reduced
- face logic is compressed
- creatures are built from few clear units
- symbols are stated bluntly
This is because simplification makes room for:
- substitution
- visual humor
- symbolic charge
- animation logic
- repeated motifs
- dream readability
## Good simplicity
Good simplicity is:
- intentional
- legible
- graphic
- rude
- compressed
- transformable
It is not:
- lazy emptiness

- flavorless minimalism
- dead vector neatness
- underdesigned blankness
## Line modes
--## 1. CONTAINMENT LINE
Used to hold:
- body glyphs
- creature silhouettes
- heads
- hands
- decorative motifs
- contour-relevant shapes
This is the most basic line mode.
--## 2. SYMBOL LINE
Used when the line itself makes the image read as:
- emblem
- icon
- ritual sign
- sticker
- warning
- parody symbol
This is especially important in mock-esoteric mode.
--## 3. MUTATION LINE
A line that implies:
- stretching
- leaking
- soft instability
- contour inheritance
- shape continuation

This is crucial for animation-aware design and still images that imply morphability.
--## 4. CHEAPNESS LINE
The line can also make the image feel:
- bootleg
- low-budget
- anti-luxury
- unserious in a good way
- raw enough to stay alive
This is one of the strongest anti-drift tools.
## Detail philosophy
Detail should be selective.
Strong places for detail:
- eyes
- teeth
- mouth cavities
- key decorative motifs
- symbolic focal points
- selected contour interruptions
- repeated glyph clusters
Weak places for detail:
- unnecessary anatomy realism
- excessive texture rendering
- polished shading logic
- refined surface finish
## Relation to LSD-hallucination logic
This style can nod toward **LSD-hallucination imagery**, but the line keeps it from dissolving
into formless over-complexity.
The line acts like a rude compression system:
- it turns visual overload into symbol
- it turns morphing into readable contour events
- it keeps significance from becoming mush

- it makes the hallucination logic cartoon-legible
So the line is one of the style’s main translation devices.
## Common failure modes
### Too polished
Line gets slick, perfect, design-school clean.
### Too weak
Forms lose readability and drift into soft color mush.
### Too detailed
Image becomes illustrative rather than symbolic.
### Too ornamental
Line becomes decorative filigree instead of graphic force.
### Too vector-corporate
Image starts looking like startup illustration or merch branding.
## Correction rules
If line is too polished:
- roughen edges slightly
- simplify anatomy
- reduce precision
- let a few things feel hand-wrong
If line is too weak:
- darken outline
- strengthen silhouette
- clarify major units
- reduce competing texture
If line is too boring:
- add one strange contour decision
- give one part of the figure mutational pressure
- interrupt symmetry
- let one body glyph dominate
## Final rule
The line in Garbage Enlightenment should feel like:

**a cartoon containment spell barely keeping the image from turning back into hallucination
soup**

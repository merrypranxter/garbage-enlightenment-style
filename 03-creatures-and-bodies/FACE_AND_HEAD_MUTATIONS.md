# FACE AND HEAD MUTATIONS
Faces and heads are among the most important transformation sites in Garbage Enlightenment.
This style does not treat the face as a stable container of identity.
It treats the face as:
- a symbolic panel

- a mutation engine
- a body-glyph cluster
- a place where identities overlap
- a site of emotional instability
- a soft border between self and symbol
## Core principle
A face should often look like it is:
- in the middle of becoming something else
- carrying more than one identity at once
- barely holding itself together
- simplified into symbolic modules
- vulnerable to substitution
## Why faces matter
Faces are useful because they can hold:
- humor
- eeriness
- pathos
- fake revelation
- grotesquerie
- tenderness
- symbolic density
They are often the fastest route to:
- emotional charge
- body glyph logic
- animation potential
- creature emergence
## Main mutation behaviors
--## 1. FEATURE REASSIGNMENT
A face may change because features are no longer loyal to their normal job.
Examples:
- eye becomes mouth
- mouth becomes cavity
- nose becomes symbol

- ear becomes decorative appendage
- face becomes mostly one giant eye
- lips behave like emblem, not speech organ
This is one of the most important mutation systems in the style.
--## 2. ASYMMETRICAL IDENTITY
Faces should often avoid perfect symmetry unless symmetry is being used strategically.
### Good asymmetries
- mismatched eyes
- uneven mouth placement
- one side of face more creature-like
- one side of face collapsing into abstraction
- split emotional expression
- unresolved contour on one side
Asymmetry creates mutational pressure.
--## 3. FACE-WITHIN-FACE
A face can contain:
- smaller faces
- implied faces
- mouth-faces
- eye-faces
- contour suggestions of secondary heads
This makes identity feel layered, recursive, and unstable.
--## 4. MULTIPLE HEADS / STACKED HEADS
Heads may:
- merge
- double
- swarm
- emerge from one another

- orbit each other
- form a collective
This is especially useful for:
- dream logic
- symbolic overload
- animation loops
- fake revelation
- LSD-like identity multiplication
Again, this is not meant as literal clinical hallucination depiction; it is a cartoon-symbolic
analogue of forms becoming too meaningful, too recursive, and too unstable.
--## 5. CONTOUR-BLEED HEADS
A head may not end where a head should end.
Its contour may:
- leak into background
- become decorative bands
- merge with hair-like abstraction
- turn into blob fields
- dissolve into rainbow topology
- mutate into creature body
This is especially useful for figure–pattern permeability.
--## 6. MASK-LIKE FACES
Some faces work best when they feel:
- emblematic
- frontal
- simplified
- poster-like
- symbolic before personal
Mask-logic is useful because it keeps the face from becoming too naturalistic.
### Good qualities
- clean central readability

- graphic intensity
- icon status
- weird stillness that can later break in motion
--## 7. PITIFUL OR EMBARRASSED HEAD ENERGY
The style often benefits from heads that are not powerful in a cool way.
Better targets:
- awkward
- goofy
- vulnerable
- slightly dumb
- struggling to stay coherent
- chemically dazed
- weirdly earnest
This helps avoid “cool dark surrealism” drift.
## Facial feature guidance
### Eyes
Can dominate the whole head or destabilize it.
### Mouths
Can become the emotional center or a gross interruption.
### Teeth
Can add comic-feral charge fast.
### Tongues
Can make the head feel immature, bodily, or mutation-soft.
### Hair / head edges
Can dissolve into:
- contour fields
- spikes
- drips
- clouds
- symbolic halos
- plasma bands

## Emotional outcomes of face mutation
Good face mutation can produce:
- freak tenderness
- goofy dread
- fake revelation
- synthetic sadness
- cosmic embarrassment
- sweet unease
- hallucinatory over-meaning
## Motion guidance
Faces in animation should often:
- change category
- trade features
- reveal hidden faces
- pulse through semi-states
- soften and sharpen cyclically
- dissolve into patterns
- re-emerge from abstraction
This is one area where the style strongly resembles **cartoonized LSD-hallucination movement
logic**:
- significance spikes suddenly
- one face implies several faces
- the image cannot hold one identity for long
- features behave like unstable symbols rather than fixed anatomy
## What to avoid
### Too portrait-like
If the face feels like a normal person with mild stylization, it is drifting.
### Too polished
If the face looks luxuriously surreal, it is drifting.
### Too monstrous in a grim way
If the face becomes a horror creature instead of a symbolic mutation site, it is drifting.
## Final rule
A face in this style should often feel like:

**a person, symbol, creature, and hallucination are all fighting politely over the same limited
amount of head-space**

# FACE PRESSURE NOTES

This file explains “face pressure” in Garbage Enlightenment:
the tendency for images, fields, blobs, and symbols to drift toward facial readability even before
becoming full faces.

This is a crucial part of the style’s emotional and hallucinatory charge.

## Core principle

A face does not need to be fully drawn to be active.

Often the strongest state is:
- almost-face
- partial-face
- face-pressure in abstraction
- misread face
- accidental face
- symbolic face waiting to happen

## WHAT FACE PRESSURE IS

Face pressure is the sense that:
- two shapes want to become eyes
- a seam wants to become a mouth
- a contour wants to become a profile
- a field wants to produce a face
- a symbol is leaning toward personhood

This can happen in:

- abstract fields
- blobs
- creature designs
- symbols
- decorative motifs
- contour bands
- swarms

## WHY IT MATTERS

Face pressure creates:
- emotional immediacy
- symbolic charge
- over-meaning
- LSD-logic adjacency
- mutational tension
- figure-pattern permeability

It is one of the main ways the style feels:
- alive
- psychologically sticky
- semi-conscious
- body-haunted

## FACE PRESSURE LEVELS

### Level 1 — slight implication
A shape vaguely hints at face organization.

### Level 2 — partial readability
One or two features almost line up into a face.

### Level 3 — unstable face
The image is readable as a face, but could still collapse back into abstraction.

### Level 4 — recursive face pressure
More than one face seems possible at once.

### Level 5 — swarm face pressure
A cluster of motifs produces many partial faces simultaneously.

## GOOD SOURCES OF FACE PRESSURE

- eyes in unusual placements
- dark seam that could be mouth
- paired contour bulges
- asymmetrical blobs
- field bands that create cheek/forehead logic

- decorative patterns that imply head structure
- creature forms that are mostly face

## BAD FACE PRESSURE

### Too clean
Becomes a standard portrait.

### Too random
Becomes meaningless pareidolia without style logic.

### Too horror-realistic
Becomes grim face distortion instead of symbolic pressure.

### Too resolved
Loses the unstable almost-state that gives it charge.

## FACE PRESSURE + LSD-LOGIC

This is one of the clearest structural parallels to **cartoonized LSD-hallucination logic**:
- faces emerging where they are not fully there
- one image implying several faces
- significance arriving through partial recognition
- abstraction gaining personhood too quickly

Again, the point is not realism.
The point is symbolic instability.

## PROMPT PHRASES

Useful phrases:
- latent face pressure
- almost-face contour logic
- partial facial readability
- abstraction leaning toward portrait
- recursive face pressure
- body-haunted facial emergence
- emotionally charged pareidolia in cartoon form

## FINAL RULE

A strong Garbage Enlightenment image often feels like:
**the face is not fully present yet, but the image is already thinking about becoming one**

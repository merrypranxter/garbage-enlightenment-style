# BODY GLYPHS
In Garbage Enlightenment, body parts are not merely anatomical components.
They are symbolic units.
A mouth is not just a mouth.
An eye is not just an eye.
A hand is not just a hand.
These forms behave like:
- icons
- emblems
- stickers
- portals
- warnings
- jokes
- totems
- ritual props
- decorative modules
- metamorphic seeds
## Core principle
Body parts should be treated as **graphic-symbolic matter**.
They may appear:
- detached
- repeated
- mutated
- hybridized
- enlarged
- simplified
- decorative

- embedded inside other forms
- used as stand-ins for emotion, revelation, threat, humor, or absurdity
## Why body glyphs matter
They are one of the fastest ways this style communicates:
- bodily charge
- symbolic weirdness
- comic unease
- dream logic
- fake-sacred energy
- visual recognition
- transformation potential
They also help bridge:
- figurative work
- abstract work
- occult parody
- cute corruption
- animation logic
## Primary glyph families
--## 1. EYES
Eyes are one of the most important glyphs in the whole style.
### Possible roles
- watcher
- portal
- logo
- wound
- ornament
- surveillance symbol
- spiritual joke
- false revelation
- creature seed
- abstract pattern node
### Visual behaviors
- floating alone
- stacked

- embedded in other body parts
- used inside flowers, hearts, blobs, rainbows, mouths, or heads
- looped recursively
- enlarged into central icons
- multiplied across surfaces
- distorted into almond, circular, slit, or portal-like forms
### Emotional charge
Eyes can feel:
- paranoid
- stupid
- cosmic
- fake-profound
- cute
- accusatory
- dreamy
- absurd
--## 2. MOUTHS / LIPS
Mouths are one of the style’s most flexible symbols.
### Possible roles
- joke
- entry point
- cavity
- speechless scream
- sensual fake-symbol
- sticker emblem
- gross decoration
- soft body ornament
- threshold between states
### Visual behaviors
- floating lips
- mouths with no face
- mouths inside eyes
- mouths on creatures
- teeth-heavy mouth cavities
- simple lipstick-like cartoon mouth shapes
- repeating mouth motifs
- lip-shapes used as abstract units

### Emotional charge
Mouths can be:
- seductive
- dumb
- gross
- decorative
- comic
- ominous
- embarrassing
- synthetic
- weirdly tender
--## 3. TEETH
Teeth intensify images fast.
They are useful because they can make something look:
- funny
- threatening
- pathetic
- feral
- demented
- toy-like
- body-horror-ish
- stupid in a great way
### Visual behaviors
- exposed rows
- mismatched spacing
- too many teeth
- tiny cartoon nibs
- jagged grin inserts
- teeth appearing where they do not belong
- mouth interiors simplified into black cavities with bright tooth accents
### Good use
Teeth should usually support:
- comic grotesque
- mutation
- personality distortion
- bodily emphasis

Rather than drifting too far into realistic gore.
--## 4. TONGUES
Tongues are perfect for this style because they sit between:
- body humor
- grossness
- softness
- erotic parody
- creature weirdness
- symbolic absurdity
### Visual behaviors
- dangling
- elongated
- simplified ribbon-tongues
- emerging from non-mouth spaces
- acting like tendrils
- used as graphic shapes
- interacting with eyes or cavities
- serving as mutation bridges
Tongues can make an image feel:
- immature
- bodily
- playful
- nasty
- funny
- soft-gross
--## 5. HANDS
Hands are extremely important because they can function as:
- glyphs
- signs
- warnings
- prayers
- mock spiritual symbols
- ritual objects
- body emblems

- palmistry jokes
- flat iconic units
### Visual behaviors
- floating hands
- front-facing palm symbols
- symmetrical hand displays
- simplified finger anatomy
- glowing or aura-ringed hands
- hands as stand-alone emblematic panels
- hands merged with symbols, eyes, drips, or portals
### Emotional charge
Hands can feel:
- sacred-looking
- fake-sacred
- cartoon-serious
- ritualistic
- vulnerable
- symbolic
- stupidly profound
They are especially powerful in mock-esoteric compositions.
--## 6. HEADS / FACES
Faces in this style are rarely stable.
They are often:
- split
- merged
- doubled
- asymmetrical
- badly contained
- stacked
- leaking
- hosting extra features
- turning into creatures
- fragmenting into symbolic modules
### Common visual behaviors
- two profiles merging
- face-within-face

- multiple heads attached to one form
- heads emerging from bodies or objects
- facial planes simplified into graphic regions
- mismatched eyes and mouths
- expressions sliding between funny and disturbed
### Good emotional target
Faces should often feel like:
- identities in trouble
- symbols pretending to be people
- people pretending to be stickers
- dream selves caught mid-mutation
--## 7. SKULLS
Skulls are useful because they instantly bring:
- mortality
- cartoon death
- cosmic emptiness
- punk/occult energy
- fake seriousness
- simple iconic readability
### Visual behaviors
- simplified skulls
- floating skull emblems
- skull-as-space-object
- skull embedded in symbol field
- skull used decoratively rather than anatomically
- skulls in cute or absurd contexts
### Tone guidance
Skulls do not need to be grim.
They can be:
- funny
- toy-like
- iconic
- sweetly eerie
- cosmic
- decorative
- fake-deep

--## 8. EARS
Ears are underrated in this style.
They’re weird, soft, bodily, and immediately a little wrong when isolated.
### Good uses
- floating detached ears
- ears on unexpected creatures
- exaggerated ear shapes
- ear as decorative body glyph
- ear in dialogue with eyes or mouths
Ears add a nice “what the fuck is this doing here” energy.
--## 9. FEET / LIMBS
Feet and limbs often appear as:
- stumpy
- symbolic
- toy-like
- weak
- awkward
- detached
- simplified into little cartoon supports
This helps keep figures from becoming too heroic or anatomically resolved.
They’re useful for:
- pathetic creature energy
- anti-elegance
- crude bodily comedy
--## 10. ORGANIC BLOBS / FLESH SHAPES
Some body glyphs are not specific organs at all.
They are just:
- soft flesh-like forms
- cavities

- drips
- lumps
- sacs
- goo regions
- bulbous living masses
These act as pre-symbolic body matter.
They are especially useful as transition material in morphing and animation.
## Body glyph behaviors in animation
Body glyphs are especially powerful in motion because they can:
- swap roles quickly
- emerge from other forms
- repeat in loops
- drift between decorative and anatomical function
- become other glyphs
- appear briefly as intermediate states
Examples:
- eye becomes mouth
- mouth becomes portal
- hand becomes aura-icon
- head becomes repeating swarm
- tongue becomes ribbon-shape
- skull emerges from contour field
## Body glyphs and Sweet Corruption
Body glyphs often become more stylistically potent when paired with cute or decorative motifs.
Examples:
- eye inside a star
- rainbow with teeth
- heart with a mouth
- cute creature with a hand-glyph body
- sticker sweetness containing body weirdness
This is strongly encouraged.
## What to avoid
### Weak use
- realistic anatomy studies

- clean anatomical correctness
- boring portrait naturalism
- body parts used only literally
- standard medical illustration vibes
### Drift risk
If body glyphs become too realistic, the style loses its symbolic cartoon charge.
## Final rule
A body part in this style should usually be doing at least one extra job beyond “being anatomy.”
It should also function as:
- symbol
- joke
- icon
- warning
- mutation seed
- decorative unit
- portal
- glyph
That extra job is where the style wakes up.

# SYMBOLIC ROLE MATRIX

This file maps common motifs to the symbolic roles they can play inside Garbage
Enlightenment.

The point is to help prompts, systems, and curation avoid treating motifs as fixed nouns.

## Core principle

A motif is stronger when it can perform multiple symbolic roles.

Example:
An eye is not just “an eye.”
It can be:
- watcher
- portal
- logo
- warning
- joke
- creature seed
- sacred parody icon

## ROLE CATEGORIES

### Body
The motif behaves as anatomy or body matter.

### Symbol
The motif behaves like an emblem, sign, or icon.

### Creature
The motif becomes part of or all of a being.

### Decoration
The motif functions as stylistic filler or sweet/gross accent.

### Portal
The motif suggests threshold, entry, cavity, or cosmic opening.

### Joke
The motif destabilizes seriousness.

### Revelation
The motif carries fake-sacred or over-significant charge.

### Mutation
The motif acts as a transition site or morph engine.

## HOW TO USE THIS MATRIX

### Prompting
Pick one motif and assign it a non-default role.

Example:

- “a rainbow functioning like body matter”
- “an eye functioning like a fake warning sign”
- “a hand functioning like a portal”
- “a heart functioning like a mouth emblem”

### Animation
Use role change as motion driver.

Example:
- eye: revelation → portal → creature
- heart: decoration → body → joke
- contour field: decoration → portal → mutation substrate

### Curation
Tag references by what roles the motif is actually playing, not just what is visible.

## LSD-LOGIC NOTE

The style feels more LSD-logic-adjacent when motifs can rapidly slide between roles.

That is one of the most useful structural cues:
**the noun is unstable because its symbolic job keeps changing**

## FINAL RULE

In Garbage Enlightenment, motifs get stronger when they are asked:
**what else can you be besides yourself?**

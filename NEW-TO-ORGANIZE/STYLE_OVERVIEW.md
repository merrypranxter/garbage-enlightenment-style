# STYLE OVERVIEW
## Name
**Garbage Enlightenment**
### Subtitle: **Sweet Corruption Acid Cartoon Surrealism**
This is the working name for a style defined by low-fi psychedelic cartoon mutation, bodily
symbolism, cute contamination, parody-esoteric imagery, and rapid metamorphic animation.
## One-sentence definition
A flat, low-fi psychedelic cartoon style where cute symbols, bodily glyphs, occult jokes, mutant
creatures, and abstract hallucination fields animate through continuous symbolic
metamorphosis.
## Expanded definition
Garbage Enlightenment is a style of visual thinking in which:
- bodies are broken into symbols
- symbols behave like cartoon matter
- cartoon matter is unstable
- instability is part of the beauty
- sweetness and grossness are allowed to coexist
- occult imagery is treated as unstable theater rather than serious doctrine

- motion is based on transformation, not realism
- abstract psychedelic fields and figurative mutant drawings belong to the same visual
ecosystem
This style feels like a prankster counterfeit of revelation.
It borrows visual charge from:
- psychedelia
- outsider cartooning
- grotesque pop
- underground animation
- low-fi digital weirdness
- conspiracy / occult iconography
- cutesy sticker language
- bodily surrealism
- cheap shader hallucination
But it does not fully belong to any of those categories.
## What the style feels like
This style can feel like:
- a stupid cosmic warning
- a fake sacred cartoon
- a body part pretending to be a logo
- a rainbow hallucination made of bad decisions
- a cute sticker infected by mutation
- a conspiracy symbol that got too high to stay menacing
- a dream that keeps changing what it is while you’re still looking at it
It is often playful, weird, and synthetic.
It can be bodily and gross, but not necessarily cruel.
It can be ominous, but rarely solemn.
It can be cute, but rarely pure.
## Core ingredients
### Flat cartoon simplification
The style tends toward graphic flatness rather than heavy rendering.
### Rude outlines
Thin-to-medium black outlines often define the whole image.

### Synthetic color
Colors feel artificial, posterized, candy-like, acid, cheap, loud, or chemically fake.
### Bodily symbolism
Eyes, mouths, teeth, tongues, hands, ears, skulls, and heads are used as core visual signs.
### Cute contamination
Stars, rainbows, hearts, playful animals, sticker-like motifs, and decorative sweetness are
allowed in the same space as body weirdness.
### Mock esoterica
Pyramids, all-seeing eyes, portals, sigil-ish shapes, and fake ritual symbols often appear, but
with irony, absurdity, or cartoon disrespect.
### Metamorphic logic
Forms tend to become other forms rather than merely posing.
### Abstract hallucination space
Some compositions are fully non-figurative: contour maps, rainbow melts, oil-slick bands, or
liquid topologies.
## Emotional range
This style is not locked into one mood.
It can validly occupy multiple states:
- cute absurdity
- sweet body weirdness
- gross-funny mutation
- parody-occult menace
- dreamy synthetic wonder
- dumb cosmic dread
- full freak intensity
The style works best when it retains tonal instability.
## What this style is not
It is not:
- polished visionary art
- ornate psychedelic poster filigree
- clean kawaii
- slick vector editorial illustration

- grim body horror
- luxury pop surrealism
- generic AI “trippy rainbow” slop
- solemn occult iconography
- corporate playful illustration
- fantasy-animal poster art played straight
## Main visual tension
The central tension of the style is:
**sweetness contaminated by weirdness**
and sometimes
**weirdness softened by sweetness**
This is not accidental.
It is one of the defining laws.
## Main motion tension
The central movement tension of the style is:
**one thing still being itself while becoming something else**
That is why loops, in-betweens, substitutions, and contour mutations matter so much.
## Informal motto
This style should feel like:
**a cute cosmic lie told through bad cartoon metamorphosis**

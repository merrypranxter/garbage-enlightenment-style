# POSTER COMPOSITION NOTES

This file focuses on poster-like and print-like composition logic for Garbage Enlightenment.

This is useful because many outputs in this style naturally want to behave like:
- posters
- stickers
- shirt graphics
- icon prints
- symbolic placards
- fake revelation notices
- cursed little decorative announcements

## Core principle

Poster composition in this style should prioritize:
- immediate symbolic read
- strong central force
- rude flatness

- graphic hierarchy
- room for contamination
- anti-polish punch
- potential for motion implication even when static

## CORE POSTER MODES

---

## 1. SINGLE CENTRAL SYMBOL POSTER

### Description
One large body glyph, creature, or fake sacred icon dominates the frame.

### Best subjects
- hand
- eye
- face
- heart-mouth
- skull
- body goblin
- eye pyramid
- portal symbol

### Why it works
- fast readability
- strong graphic punch
- easy to print
- very on-brand
- easy to contaminate with little motifs

### Best support
- flat field
- halo
- contour-band aura
- orbiting stars/hearts/eyes

---

## 2. SYMBOL SHEET POSTER

### Description
Multiple icons share the frame in a sticker-sheet or emblem catalog arrangement.

### Best for
- motif studies
- style overviews
- body glyph packs

- fake esoteric sheets
- cute corruption arrays

### Good compositional rule
Use repetition with variation.
Do not make each unit too unrelated.

---

## 3. FAKE REVELATION POSTER

### Description
The image feels like a low-budget cosmic sermon, warning, or revelation graphic.

### Good ingredients
- central icon
- halo or portal
- eye logic
- stars or symbolic clutter
- field pressure
- fake significance

### Tone
Should feel:

- charged
- a little stupid
- slightly bodily
- fake sacred
- visually overcommitted

---

## 4. CREATURE PORTRAIT POSTER

### Description
A little freak creature occupies a poster-like stage, usually with simple support motifs.

### Best for
- body goblins
- face-creatures
- cute-corrupted animals
- weird mascot-like beings

### Rule
The creature should still feel iconically readable, not scenic.

---

## 5. ABSTRACT PRESSURE POSTER

### Description
A mostly abstract hallucination field is treated like a poster image, with one or more implied
symbolic emergence zones.

### Good ingredients
- contour bands
- thermal maps
- body-glyph hints
- central pressure zone
- synthetic rude palette

### Danger
Can become pure wallpaper.
Must preserve emergence pressure.

## HIERARCHY RULES

Good poster hierarchy often includes:
- one dominant object
- one secondary contamination layer
- one supporting atmosphere layer
- restrained repetition

### Weak hierarchy
- everything same size
- clutter with no focal point
- decorative overload
- no symbol dominance

## EDGES AND CORNERS

Use corners for:
- stars
- little eyes
- bubbles
- repeated motifs
- fake sigils
- supportive sticker clutter

Do not let the corners become too elegant or filigreed.
Keep them blunt and slightly rude.

## NEGATIVE SPACE

Poster compositions often benefit from:
- flat open zones
- breathing room around symbols

- room for fake importance

Negative space helps:
- cheap poster authority
- icon clarity
- emotional weirdness

## LSD-LOGIC NOTE

Poster composition can still encode **cartoonized LSD-hallucination logic** if:
- one symbol implies multiple symbolic states
- the field feels semantically pressurized
- repetition suggests over-meaning
- the central image seems like it could morph into another reading

The poster should feel simple enough to read fast, but weird enough to keep mutating in the
mind.

## FINAL RULE

A Garbage Enlightenment poster should feel like:
**a cheap, over-significant, body-weird announcement from a cartoon metaphysical
bureaucracy**

LOOP_TIMING_NOTES.md
# LOOP TIMING NOTES

This file focuses on timing behavior in Garbage Enlightenment loops, GIFs, and animations.

This matters because the style’s identity is heavily tied to:
- morph cadence
- intermediate-state value
- cheap dream timing
- symbolic substitution speed
- recurrence behavior

## Core principle

Good loop timing in this style is not about:
- cinematic realism
- polished character performance
- elegant motion arcs
- silky seamlessness for its own sake

It is about:
- symbolic pressure
- transformation rhythm
- weird recurrence

- semi-state aliveness
- slightly wrong timing that still feels inevitable

## PRIMARY TIMING QUALITIES

---

## 1. QUICK SEMANTIC SHIFTS

The style often benefits from identity changes happening quickly enough to feel surprising but
clearly enough to be legible.

Good timing effect:
- you notice a form becoming something else almost too late
- significance reassigns itself abruptly
- cute becomes bodily quickly
- symbol becomes creature before fully stabilizing

### Important note
This is why analyzing every 5 seconds is too slow for this style.
Important change can happen in 1–2 second windows or faster.

---

## 2. HELD ALMOST-STATES

The loop sometimes gets extra power by pausing briefly on:
- half-face
- almost-eye
- almost-mouth
- symbol-creature hybrids
- contour states that could still go either way

These brief holds can make the loop feel:
- charged
- eerie
- over-significant
- weirdly alive

---

## 3. CHEAP DREAM CADENCE

The timing should sometimes feel:
- a little low-budget
- slightly possessed
- not professionally smoothed
- dream-looped
- more insistent than polished

This is a feature, not a defect.

---

## 4. RECURRENCE WITH SLIGHT DIFFERENCE

A form may return, but altered.
This is stronger than a perfectly clean reset.

Examples:
- eye returns slightly more mouth-like
- rainbow returns slightly body-haunted
- creature returns with different feature emphasis
- field resets but keeps a symbolic aftertaste

This helps preserve mutational continuity.

---

## 5. RHYTHMIC PRESSURE

Some loops should feel like:
- pulsing

- breathing
- ritual insistence
- pressure building and releasing
- symbolic heartbeats
- revelation trying and failing to land

This is especially useful for:
- eyes
- hands
- halo icons
- contour fields
- portal images

## TIMING STRUCTURES

### Structure A — fast morph / brief hold / collapse
Good for:
- icon loops
- body glyph substitutions
- eye-mouth transformations

### Structure B — slow emergence / quick reveal / dissolve
Good for:
- field-to-figure emergence

- fake revelation
- abstract pressure loops

### Structure C — wobble / symbolic spike / reset
Good for:
- body goblins
- pathetic creature loops
- sweet corruption critters

### Structure D — pulse / split / merge / altered return
Good for:
- swarm logic
- repeated motif systems
- recursive symbols

## BAD TIMING MODES

### Too smooth
Feels over-produced and dead.

### Too slow
Loses symbolic volatility.

### Too random

Feels meaningless rather than mutational.

### Too perfectly looped
Can become sterile.

### Too action-focused
Misses the metamorphic essence.

## LSD-LOGIC NOTE

Loop timing becomes more Garbage Enlightenment-specific when it supports **cartoonized
LSD-hallucination logic**:
- rapid symbolic reassignment
- surprise emergence of alternate readings
- semi-stable visual identities
- over-meaning arriving in pulses
- forms becoming too significant too fast

This is not about simulating literal pharmacology.
It is about timing symbolic instability correctly.

## FINAL RULE

A good Garbage Enlightenment loop should feel like:
**it keeps realizing something about itself a little too fast, then almost forgetting it**

A pathetic little mutant creature in the Garbage Enlightenment style, with awkward anatomy,

A face-creature in the Garbage Enlightenment style, where the face behaves like both a

A cluster of tiny repeated weird creatures in the Garbage Enlightenment style, each a little
